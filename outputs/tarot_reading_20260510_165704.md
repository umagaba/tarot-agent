# Tarot Reading Report

## Your Question
was coming to plaksha university the correct decision?

## The Spread: Decision-Making Spread
A spread that evaluates past influences, present reality, future outlook, and guidance to determine the correctness of a university decision.

## The Cards

### Position 1: Past Influences
**Four of Wands** (Upright)  
Focus: Factors that led to choosing Plaksha  
Keywords:  

You entered this decision with a sense of celebration and optimism, seeing Plaksha as a place where you could build a solid foundation. The upright Four of Wands shows that past experiences of community, support, and shared success strongly attracted you. You likely felt that the university would provide a harmonious environment for both study and personal growth. This card confirms that your initial choice was rooted in genuine excitement and a desire for stability and joy.

### Position 2: Present Reality
**The Hierophant** (Upright)  
Focus: Current experience and challenges  
Keywords:  

Right now you are encountering the traditional structures and expectations of university life, feeling the pull of established curricula and institutional norms. The Hierophant upright suggests you are learning within a framework that values conformity, mentorship, and proven methods. You may be wrestling with the tension between following the prescribed path and wanting to explore your own unconventional ideas. This card invites you to respect the guidance offered while discerning which teachings truly resonate with your goals.

### Position 3: Future Outlook
**Page of Wands** (Upright)  
Focus: Potential outcomes if path continues  
Keywords:  

If you stay the course, the future holds a burst of youthful enthusiasm and creative energy for you. The upright Page of Wands signals new opportunities for exploration, fresh projects, and a willingness to take initiative. You will likely discover a sense of adventure in your studies, prompting you to experiment with innovative ideas. This card encourages you to remain curious and to let your passion guide the next steps of your academic journey.

### Position 4: Guidance
**Eight of Swords** (Reversed)  
Focus: Advice for next steps  
Keywords:  

The reversed Eight of Swords advises you to release the mental restrictions that are holding you back from seeing other options. You may be feeling trapped by self‑imposed doubts or fear of stepping outside the familiar university routine. This card urges you to challenge those limiting beliefs and consider alternative paths, whether within or beyond Plaksha. By freeing your mind, you can make a clearer, more empowered decision about your educational future.

## The Reading
You can feel the spread humming with a mix of celebration, structure, and budding enthusiasm, all underscored by a gentle urging to loosen mental shackles. The cards together paint a picture of a decision that began with genuine excitement, settled into the disciplined rhythm of university life, and now points toward a fresh, energetic phase—provided you allow yourself to think beyond the limits you’ve placed on the situation.

Your past, represented by the Four of Wands, tells us that the pull of Plaksha was rooted in a real sense of community and the promise of a stable, joyful foundation. That optimism wasn’t fleeting; it was a solid, celebratory ground that made the choice feel right at the time. The Hierophant in the present shows how that foundation has been built upon a framework of tradition, mentorship, and established expectations. You’re now navigating the conventional academic path, learning the rules and absorbing the wisdom that the institution offers, even if at moments you feel the pull of your own unconventional ideas.

Looking ahead, the Page of Wands sparks a bright, youthful energy that suggests you’ll soon encounter new projects or ideas that reignite the original excitement you felt. It’s a sign that your time at Plaksha can still be a source of creative growth and adventure, as long as you stay curious and let that inner fire guide you. The guidance card, the Eight of Swords reversed, ties everything together by reminding you that the only thing truly holding you back may be self‑imposed doubt—fear of stepping outside the familiar routine or questioning whether you’re “stuck.” By loosening those mental restraints, you open yourself to alternative possibilities, whether they remain within Plaksha’s walls or lead you elsewhere.

In short, the cards affirm that coming to Plaksha was a sound decision based on solid, joyful foundations, and the university continues to offer valuable structure. Yet they also encourage you to keep your mind open, release the limiting narratives you’ve built, and let the fresh enthusiasm of the Page of Wands guide any next steps. So, yes—your choice was correct, and the path forward will be most rewarding when you allow yourself the freedom to explore beyond the mental fences you’ve erected.

## Actionable Takeaways
Reflect on the community and supportive moments that initially attracted you to Plaksha; revisit those activities or groups to rekindle that sense of joy.  
Engage with a mentor or professor who embodies the traditional wisdom of the Hierophant, but ask probing questions to align teachings with your personal goals.  
Start a small, creative project or join a workshop that excites you, embodying the Page of Wands’ youthful energy.  
Write down any self‑doubts or perceived limitations, then actively challenge each one by researching alternative courses, internships, or extracurriculars, embodying the Eight of Swords reversed.
